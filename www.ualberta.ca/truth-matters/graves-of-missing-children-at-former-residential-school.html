Biofilter Oilsands Decontamination

Sample Article WRS210

Published first on

Folio publishes stories every weekday that highlight new research or share expertise from researchers at the University of Alberta.

Creating purposeful strategies to positively impact the environment

Dr. Mohamed Gamal El-Din is leading a study detailing a new process that can efficiently remove the main contaminants found in water used in oilsands operation..

“This method also helps speed up a process that would otherwise take decades for nature to complete,” explained Mohamed. The process involves ozonating the water to break down any organic compounds into a food source for the bacteria present in the added biofilter. The goal of this research is to provide a safe innovative approach to facilitate future water use of what was at one point contaminated unusable water.
